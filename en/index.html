Living far from home

Living abroad is a complex experience, who would understand? Being physically distant from what is familiar, missing important moments with loved ones, adapting to a new culture, communicating in another language, creating a new support network — it's crossing both a literal and symbolic ocean of emotions.

Anxiety, loneliness, helplessness, among many other difficulties can be part of this process. Psychotherapy offers a human, safe, and ethical space to embrace these experiences, understand their impacts, and create strategies to deal with them in a more balanced way, promoting well-being.

About me

Graduated in Psychology from the Federal University of São Carlos (UFSCar) in 2017, with emphasis on Neuroscience and Behavior Analysis, I discovered in college a passion that would take me far beyond borders.

At 20, I completed my first academic exchange, studying for two semesters at the University of Porto in Portugal. After graduation, I spent several years between clinical care and organizational psychology until a professional opportunity took me to Berlin, Germany, where I worked at a large multinational for two years. Currently, I am pursuing a double degree master's program, partly in Spain and partly in Italy, which has allowed me to deepen my knowledge in social psychology, as well as rekindling my interest in clinical practice.

About the approach

Online therapy with a human and integrative approach, combining principles of Behavior Analysis, Social Psychology, and Neuroscience. I use evidence-based techniques to understand behavioral patterns, helping to promote effective and lasting changes. Each intervention is carefully adapted to your needs, taking into account the complexity of the emotional and cultural experiences you're going through.

All treatment is conducted with respect for professional ethics and absolute confidentiality, creating a safe and welcoming space where you can explore and understand your challenges with confidence and clarity.

Therapy will help you:

Deal with loneliness and lack of emotional connection in a new country

Understand and embrace the emotional impact of cultural adaptation

Reduce anxiety generated by changes and uncertainties

Strengthen your identity amid so many external changes

Work through communication and expression difficulties in another language or culture

Create space for self-care amid the demands of life abroad

Frequently Asked Questions

How does online therapy work?

Sessions are conducted via secure video conferencing, lasting 50 minutes, adapted to your time zone.

What are the session fees?

Fees vary according to the country where you reside. Contact me to discuss available options.

Does my international health insurance cover the sessions?

I work with reimbursement for various international plans. I can provide the necessary receipts for your reimbursement.

How often should I attend therapy?

Weekly sessions are recommended, especially at the beginning of the process. Over time, we can adjust the frequency according to your needs.

How long does the therapeutic process last?

The duration varies according to each person and their goals. Some benefit from shorter processes (3-6 months), while others prefer longer-term support. We'll evaluate together the best path for you.

How do I know if online therapy is effective?

Scientific research shows that online therapy has comparable efficacy to in-person therapy. The key lies in the quality of the therapeutic connection, which can be established regardless of format.
